## What happened

On February 6, 2025, Andrej Karpathy — former OpenAI founding team, former Tesla AI director, and one of the most influential KOLs in AI — posted on X:

> "There's a new kind of coding I call 'vibe coding', where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists. It's possible because the LLMs (e.g. Cursor Composer w Sonnet) are getting too good. Also I just talk to Composer with SuperWhisper..."

**The result**: **4.5M+ views**. Picked up by global tech press — NYT, Ars Technica, The Guardian all wrote independent stories. Nine months later, **Collins Dictionary added "vibe coding" as a formal entry**.

Karpathy himself later wrote: "This was a shower-thoughts throwaway tweet. I didn't expect it to blow up."

## Why this is the most cost-effective borrowed authority in GTM

List what Cursor walked away with:

1. **Concept ownership.** "Vibe coding" is now permanently anchored to Cursor. Anyone who later uses the term defaults to Cursor as the implicit referent.
2. **Cross-segment penetration.** From dev-Twitter to mainstream press. NYT readers don't write code, but they've heard of Cursor.
3. **Dictionary inclusion.** Collins Dictionary entry = entering the language itself. **A permanent asset that no marketing budget can buy.**
4. **Tied to Sonnet.** The tweet names Cursor *and* Anthropic's Sonnet. Two top-tier brands cross-validating each other.

**How much did Cursor pay for this?** $0. Karpathy isn't a Cursor investor, isn't a paid KOL, has no commercial relationship with the company. This is pure borrowed authority.

## How "happenstance" became "high-probability event"

Karpathy says he didn't expect it to blow up — true. But the tweet *landing on Cursor specifically* is **not random**.

Look at the pre-conditions for Karpathy choosing Cursor + Sonnet as his example:

1. **Cursor's position in Karpathy's mental model.** Karpathy almost certainly watched Lex Fridman #447 (Oct 2024) — he's a frequent Lex listener, and that episode featured all four Cursor founders. Karpathy now has a deep impression of who they are.
2. **Cursor was the de facto standard for AI editors at the time.** As of Feb 2025, what was Karpathy actually using? If he'd been on GitHub Copilot, the example in the tweet would have been "Copilot," not "Cursor Composer."
3. **Composer's voice integration.** The tweet specifically calls out "talk to Composer with SuperWhisper." That specific use case — voice-driven coding in conversation — was best supported in Cursor Composer at that time.

Translate: **when Karpathy reached for a concrete example of "vibe coding," Cursor was the only tool that came naturally to mind.**

That isn't luck. That's **18 months of mind-share investment** with the kind of people whose tweets shape mind-share — Lex Fridman, the dev-Twitter elite, "engineers at OpenAI / Midjourney use it" social proof. All of it combined to make a single fact true: **when someone of Karpathy's stature looks for a representative AI coding example, Cursor is the default answer.**

## The compounding value of "concept ownership"

"Vibe coding" became its own propagation unit after the tweet. Every subsequent person who:

- Wrote about AI coding → used "vibe coding" as the framing
- Made an AI coding demo video → put "vibe coding" in the title
- Argued against AI auto-coding → was arguing against "vibe coding"
- Even Karpathy himself — later distinguished "vibe coding" from "AI-assisted coding"

…handed Cursor a free brand exposure each time, because Cursor is the implicit referent built into the term's origin story. **Cursor doesn't own the word. Cursor owns the "origin context" of the word.**

## Timing was load-bearing

Note when this dropped: **Feb 6, 2025** — Cursor had just crossed $100M ARR (in January). The timing matters:

- **If this had been June 2024** (Cursor at low single-digit-millions ARR) — Karpathy probably wouldn't have used Cursor as his example. Cursor wasn't yet the obvious default.
- **If this had been June 2025** (Cursor already at $500M ARR + Series C) — Karpathy choosing Cursor would be obvious; the tweet's "discovery" novelty would be lower.
- **Feb 2025 is the Goldilocks zone**: well-known enough to be picked, not yet dominant enough to be boring.

This timing match isn't something Cursor designed — but the **continuous narrative investment** meant Cursor was the best available choice at any given timing.
